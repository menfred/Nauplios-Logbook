# Privacy Policy for Nauplios Logbook
Last Updated: April 5, 2026

This privacy policy explains how N0PL105 Log Book ("Nauplios", "the App", "we", "us") handles your data. Our core philosophy is Offline First & Local Storage. We believe that your sailing logs, routes, and photos belong solely to you.

## 1. Data Collection and Storage
No Cloud Sync (Yet): The App stores all your data (including log entries, charter vessel details, photos, and location history) locally on your device using Apple's native SwiftData framework. We do not transmit, back up, or store your data on our own servers or any third-party servers.

## 2. Device Sensors & Permissions
To provide accurate maritime tracking, the App requests access to specific device sensors:

Location (GPS): We use CoreLocation to fetch your current coordinates, speed (SOG), and calculate Course Over Ground (COG). This data is strictly saved to your local logbook entries. We do not track you in the background unless explicitly required by an active voyage tracking feature, and we never share your location data.

Camera & Photo Library: We request access to your camera and photo library so you can attach images to your log entries and charter vessel profiles. These images are stored locally in the App's database.

Motion Sensors (Barometer): We use CoreMotion to read atmospheric pressure (hPa) for your weather logs. This data is entirely processed on your device.

## 3. Analytics and Third-Party Tracking
The App contains no third-party tracking SDKs, no advertisements, and no analytics tools (like Google Analytics or Facebook Pixel). We do not collect anonymous usage statistics. What happens on your boat, stays on your boat.

## 4. Exporting Your Data
You have full control over your data. You can export your voyages at any time via the App's Export Manager (as PDF, GPX, or CSV) using iOS's native Share Sheet.

## 5. Changes to This Policy
If we introduce optional features in the future (such as Apple iCloud synchronization or CloudKit backups), this policy will be updated to reflect that you must explicitly opt-in to those services.
